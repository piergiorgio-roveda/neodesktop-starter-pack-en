# neoDesktop — starter-pack (EN)

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Do not `git push` to this public repository.** It is the shared *template* for everyone,
> not your personal workspace. To adopt your own neoDesktop:
> 1. Create your **own private repository** and **clone/rebuild** this pack into it
>    (not "Fork": a fork on GitHub stays **public** — your data would be exposed).
> 2. Personalize it with `/init` **on your private repo**.
> 3. When you want to activate the channel, **add `piergiorgio-roveda` as a collaborator to
>    YOUR private repo** — do not request access to this one.

> **Versione italiana:** https://github.com/piergiorgio-roveda/neodesktop-starter-pack-it

This is the **public starter-pack** of a neoDesktop: a workspace with an AI agent, made of a
folder, a few plain-text documents that describe how you work, and an agent (Claude Code)
that reads them and works inside your process.

It is not an app and it is not a subscription you log into. It is a **starting point you adopt
on your own**: you build your own copy, it is **yours**, and it lives on your computer and in
your repository. Nothing is pushed onto you.

## Start here — read the PROVENANCE

Before anything else, read **[`PROVENANCE.en.md`](PROVENANCE.en.md)** (it is also available
as a PDF). It is the heart of this pack and it does two things:

- it **explains** where every file comes from and what it does, and
- it **guides you to rebuild** the whole space step by step, with your own Claude Code,
  **without even cloning** this repository.

That second part is the point: you can become the author of your files instead of receiving
them from someone else.

## How to adopt it — at your own pace

1. **Discover** — you are here (or you received the PROVENANCE PDF).
2. **Understand** — read the PROVENANCE calmly. There is no rush.
3. **Build** — two paths, your choice:
   - **clone** this repository, or
   - **rebuild it** from scratch, guided by the PROVENANCE, with your own Claude Code.
4. **Personalize** — open the folder with Visual Studio Code, open Claude Code, run
   `/init`: it interviews you and writes everything in your working language.
5. **Activate the channel with Piergiorgio** — when you are ready, **you** are the one who
   creates a **private** repository for your space and invites **`piergiorgio-roveda`** as a
   collaborator. From that moment the message loop is active: you open the door.

No step is urgent: take the time to understand each part. If something is unclear, open a
**public issue** on this repository (see below).

## The commands

- `/init` — the first setup (one time only).
- `/message-to-pj` — write a message to Piergiorgio.
- `/message-from-pj` — read Piergiorgio's messages and reply.

The two message commands become useful once you have created your private repository and
invited Piergiorgio.

## Where things are

- `PROVENANCE.en.md` — start here: provenance map + rebuild guide.
- `CLAUDE.md` — the "contract": who you are and how you work. The agent always reads it.
- `context/` — your memory: profile, clients, projects.
- `communications/` — the messages with Piergiorgio.

## Questions?

Open an issue on this repository. Issues are **public by choice**: that is what makes the
